# TeaTiers Enrichment LLM: Gemini Free Tier vs. Free Alternatives vs. YandexGPT — Verification & Ranking

## TL;DR
- **Revise the plan: do NOT run Gemini's *free* tier as the primary enrichment LLM from a German/EU egress.** Google's Gemini API Additional Terms (effective March 23, 2026) state verbatim: *"You may use only Paid Services when making API Clients available to users in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom,"* and apply the Paid data terms to **all** services for EEA/CH/UK-based accounts. Output storage/re-serving itself is fine (Google explicitly disclaims ownership of generated content), but the "free, no-card" premise breaks the moment the account/users are tied to the EU.
- **The best realistically free-reachable primary for ru/zh tea naming is DeepSeek V4 Flash** (5M free tokens on signup, no card; output rights "assigned to you"; best-in-class Chinese). **Groq** is the best truly-perpetual no-card free fallback (weak on Chinese); **Mistral** requires phone verification and is weak on Chinese.
- **YandexGPT Lite is CONFIRMED safe as the always-works no-VPN safety net:** its terms explicitly permit storing and re-serving output inside your own commercial product, training can be disabled via a logging opt-out, and it supports JSON-schema structured output.

## Key Findings

### 1. Gemini free tier from the EU (decisive)
- **No card globally, no expiry — but model-restricted:** A Gemini API key can be created in Google AI Studio without a credit card, and the free tier does not expire. As of mid-2026 the free-eligible models are **Flash and Flash-Lite only** (`gemini-2.5-flash`, `gemini-2.5-flash-lite`, `gemini-3-flash`, `gemini-3.1-flash-lite`); Pro models moved to paid-only around April 1, 2026. Google's billing doc confirms: *"New accounts begin on the Free Tier, which allows access to certain models in the Gemini API and AI Studio, up to the models' free tier rate limits."*
- **The EEA blocker (official, decisive):** The Gemini API Additional Terms state verbatim: *"You may use only Paid Services when making API Clients available to users in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom."* And separately: *"If you're in the European Economic Area, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom, the terms under 'How Google uses Your Data' in 'Paid Services' apply to all Services, including Google AI Studio and unpaid quota in the Gemini API, even though they are offered free of charge."*
- **Interpretation for TeaTiers:** The split-tunnel VPN gives a German IP, but the restriction keys on **user/account geography, not egress IP**. If the TeaTiers app serves EEA users, or the Google account is operated from/registered in the EEA, the Paid-data clause attaches and free-tier production use is non-compliant. The historical "free tier not available in the EEA/UK/CH" restriction has **not** been lifted — it persists in the current terms as a Paid-Services-required clause.
- **Free-tier limits are NOT officially pinned to a single table:** Google's official rate-limits page now says limits are per-project (not per-key) and directs you to AI Studio for live values, warning *"Specified rate limits are not guaranteed and actual capacity may vary."* Secondary trackers report roughly 10–15 RPM, 250–1,500 RPD, and up to 1M TPM for Flash/Flash-Lite as of mid-2026, but these conflict across sources owing to December 2025 and April 2026 quota cuts. **Treat any specific RPM/RPD as unconfirmed.**

### 2. Gemini output-storage ToS
- **Ownership / re-serving — permitted:** *"Some of our Services allow you to generate original content. Google won't claim ownership over that content. You acknowledge that Google may generate the same or similar content for others and that we reserve all rights to do so."* You may store and commercially re-serve output; both free and paid tiers permit commercial use. You must comply with applicable law (which "may require the provision of attribution to your users when returned as part of an API call").
- **Training on free data — yes, and only disableable by going paid:** *"When you use Unpaid Services… Google uses the content you submit to the Services and any generated responses to provide, improve, and develop Google products and services and machine learning technologies… To help with quality and improve our products, human reviewers may read, annotate, and process your API input and output."* On the paid tier this stops: *"When you use Paid Services… Google doesn't use your prompts… or responses to improve our products."* **Key twist:** an EEA/CH/UK-based account gets the Paid data-handling (no training) applied to all services automatically — so for an EU developer the training concern is mooted, but only because the EEA clause forces the paid data regime (and, for serving EU users, billing).

### 3. DeepSeek (zh booster)
- **Free allowance — one-time bonus, NOT perpetual:** New accounts receive **5 million free tokens on signup, no credit card**, automatically applied across all models. This is a **one-time grant that expires 30 days from registration** (the timer starts at registration, not first use); one grant per account. At V4-Flash rates the grant is worth roughly $8–$10. After it is consumed, you pay per token.
- **Pricing after the bonus (official, June 2026):** DeepSeek **V4 Flash** = **$0.0028/M cache-hit, $0.14/M cache-miss input, $0.28/M output** (the 98% cache discount took effect 2026-04-26). Endpoint: `https://api.deepseek.com` (OpenAI-compatible; Anthropic-compatible at `https://api.deepseek.com/anthropic`).
- **Signup/payment friction:** Email, phone, or Google/Apple sign-in. Phone SMS verification can fail outside China and historically required a +86 number, but **Google sign-in works internationally**, so a Russian phone or card is not required to create the account. Top-ups (only needed after the free bonus) accept PayPal, card, Alipay, WeChat Pay.
- **Output ToS — you own and may re-serve it:** *"We assign any rights, title, and interests—if any—in the Outputs of the Services to you"* and *"You may apply the Inputs and Outputs of the Services to a wide range of use cases, including… derivative product development."* Storing + commercially re-serving is explicitly permitted.
- **Training:** DeepSeek may use de-identified inputs/outputs "to a minimal extent" to improve services; you can **opt out by turning off "Improve the model for everyone."**
- **China-hosting:** Hosted in China; data may be stored in mainland China. Acceptable here because tea-name strings carry no user data and egress to China is allowed.
- **zh/ru quality — strongest:** TokenMix's translation benchmark reports DeepSeek at **0.901 COMET zh→en and 0.895 en→zh**, beating GPT-5.4 (0.881/0.875) and Gemini (0.863/0.858), with a Chinese-optimized tokenizer; strong Russian. Best knowledge of correct pinyin and Chinese characters.

### 4. Groq & Mistral (free fallbacks)
- **Groq — best perpetual no-card free fallback:** Free tier, **no credit card** (sign up at console.groq.com). Per Groq's official rate-limits docs (as of June 4, 2026): `llama-3.3-70b-versatile` = 30 RPM / 1,000 RPD / 12,000 TPM / 100,000 TPD; `openai/gpt-oss-120b` = 30 RPM / 1,000 RPD / 8,000 TPM / 200,000 TPD. The most permissive free model is `llama-3.1-8b-instant` at up to **14,400 RPD**. **TPD (tokens/day) is often the true binding constraint, not RPD.** Endpoint: `https://api.groq.com/openai/v1` (OpenAI-compatible). Paid example: `openai/gpt-oss-120b` lists at $0.15/M input, $0.60/M output. Catalog is **open-weights only** (Llama, gpt-oss, Mistral Saba, Qwen, DeepSeek-R1-distill) — no first-party Chinese specialist. EU-reachable, extremely fast. Weaker on Chinese tea-name transliteration than DeepSeek/Gemini.
- **Mistral — EU-hosted but weaker zh and more friction:** Free "Experiment" tier on La Plateforme (console.mistral.ai), **no card but a verified phone number is required**; EU-hosted (Paris), GDPR-friendly. Free mode "is enabled by default with limited rate limits, intended for evaluation and prototyping." Free-tier data may be used for training; paid Pro/Team plans add a No-Telemetry/opt-out (Team is opted out by default). Russian acceptable, Chinese weaker; not a Chinese specialist. A reasonable EU fallback but **not better than DeepSeek for tea naming.**

### 5. Head-to-head ru/zh tea naming
- **Correct behavior:** 大红袍 should become **"Да Хун Пао"** — the Cyrillic transliteration of the pinyin *Dà Hóng Páo* — which is the standard Russian tea-trade name (confirmed by Russian Wikipedia and Russian tea retailers such as chay.info and liway.ru). "Большой красный халат" ("Big Red Robe") should appear **only** as a parenthetical literal gloss, never as the primary name.
- **Universal failure mode:** The common error is emitting the literal translation ("Большой красный халат") as the *name* instead of transliterating the pinyin. This must be constrained by an explicit "transliterate the pinyin into Cyrillic; do not translate the meaning" instruction plus few-shot examples, regardless of model.
- **Reasoned ranking for correct transliteration + correct pinyin + correct hanzi:**
  1. **DeepSeek** — best Chinese knowledge (correct characters + pinyin), strong Russian; most likely to know the canonical pinyin/Cyrillic for obscure teas. Risk: its reasoning can drift into Chinese; pin output language explicitly.
  2. **Gemini** — strong multilingual coverage plus the best instruction-following and structured-output enforcement, so easiest to force "transliterate-don't-translate" reliably; slightly weaker raw zh than DeepSeek.
  3. **YandexGPT** — best Cyrillic style and Russian fluency, but weaker world knowledge and Chinese; independent 2026 reviews rank YandexGPT 5 Pro/Lite near the bottom on knowledge tasks and note Chinese models handle even Russia-specific scenarios better — so it may miss correct pinyin/hanzi for less-common teas.
  4. **Groq (Llama/gpt-oss) / Mistral** — adequate Russian, weaker Chinese; higher risk of literal-translation errors and wrong characters.

### 6. Recommendation
Given the principle "free first, YandexGPT as the no-VPN safety net," the primary/fallback order is **revised** as follows:
- **Primary (free-reachable, best zh): DeepSeek V4 Flash** via the German egress. Genuinely free for the first 5M tokens (≈30 days), then trivially cheap ($0.14/$0.28 per M); output rights assigned to you; best Chinese for pinyin/hanzi accuracy.
- **Free perpetual fallback: Groq** (`llama-3.1-8b-instant` for volume, or a 70B model for quality) if you refuse any card. If you are willing to enable Gemini billing, **Gemini Flash-Lite (paid)** is the stronger fallback because billing simultaneously removes the EEA ambiguity *and* the training-on-data issue.
- **No-VPN safety net: YandexGPT Lite** — confirmed terms, JSON-schema output, always works direct from the Russia-hosted backend with Wikidata.

This ordering beats "Gemini-free as primary" because (i) Gemini's free tier is legally pushed onto Paid Services for an EU-tied commercial product, and (ii) DeepSeek is both free-on-arrival and the strongest on the core ru/zh tea-naming task.

## YandexGPT verification (no-VPN safety net)
- **Storing & re-serving output: CONFIRMED OK.** The Yandex AI Studio / Foundation Models Terms of Use (published 17 Apr 2026, effective 28 Apr 2026) state (clause 4.1) that the Customer may use the Generated Content by any lawful means consistent with the Terms; (clause 1.2.2) may **embed the generation technology into the Customer's end product**; and (clause 3.15) is treated as the **commercial end-user permitted to refine, develop, and sell its own product incorporating the Service** — the *only* commercial restriction is pure API resale/passthrough ("reselling the Service through simple API calls"). This directly resolves the prior re-serving concern that disqualified Yandex Maps.
- **Training:** Per clause 5.4.1, prompts are used for debugging/training **by default**, but logging can be disabled per on-site instructions; clause 5.5 says it takes effect **24 hours** after the opt-out, and clause 5.6 confirms that afterward prompts are not stored or used for training. Implementation: send the request header `x-data-logging-enabled: false`.
- **API/pricing:** Model URI `gpt://<folder_ID>/yandexgpt-lite/latest` (current generation YandexGPT 5 Lite); official price **$0.001667 per 1,000 tokens synchronous** (~0.20 RUB; $0.000834 async). New users without billing get **10 free prompts/hour** in the console; trial credits available on a billing account in `TRIAL_ACTIVE` status. Structured JSON output is supported via the Completions API `response_format: json_schema`.

## Recommended primary request config

**Track A — truly-free primary (DeepSeek), via German split-tunnel egress:**
- Endpoint: `https://api.deepseek.com/v1/chat/completions` (OpenAI-compatible)
- Model id: `deepseek-v4-flash` (avoid legacy alias `deepseek-chat`, which retires 2026-07-24 15:59 UTC)
- Structured output: JSON mode via `response_format: {"type": "json_object"}` (less strict than Gemini's schema enforcement — **validate against your schema in Kotlin/Spring code**, e.g. with a Jackson/`kotlinx.serialization` model + confidence-score field)
- Disable training: account settings → turn off **"Improve the model for everyone"**

**Track B — if you enable Gemini billing (recommended for robustness and to clear the EEA issue):**
- Endpoint: `https://generativelanguage.googleapis.com/v1beta/models/{model}:generateContent`
- Model id: `gemini-2.5-flash-lite` (or `gemini-3.1-flash-lite`)
- Structured output: `generationConfig.responseMimeType = "application/json"` + `responseSchema` (full JSON-Schema support including `anyOf`, `$ref`, `enum`, `propertyOrdering`; Pydantic/Zod work out-of-the-box; preserve schema key order — do not also paste the schema into the prompt)
- Training opt-out: **automatic on the paid tier** (Paid Services are not used to train); no flag needed.

**Fallback order:** **DeepSeek V4 Flash** (primary) → **Gemini Flash-Lite paid** (or **Groq `llama-3.1-8b-instant`/70B** if you refuse a card) → **YandexGPT Lite** (no-VPN safety net, direct from the Russia-hosted backend).

**Benchmarks that would change this recommendation:**
- If, on a 50–100 sample set of real Chinese tea names, DeepSeek's transliteration accuracy is not materially better than Gemini's, **promote Gemini-paid to primary** (better structured-output reliability + EEA-clean).
- If DeepSeek's China-hosted latency/`503`s exceed your enrichment SLA during peak hours, **promote Groq or Gemini-paid to primary**.
- If you decide to serve EU end-users at scale, **enable Gemini billing regardless** (the EEA Paid-Services clause makes the free tier non-compliant).

## Caveats / "uncertain — could not verify"
- **(a) Gemini EU free-tier-without-card availability:** No official Google page explicitly states that an EEA-based developer may run the *free* tier in production. The official terms instead (i) require **Paid Services** for serving EEA/CH/UK users and (ii) apply the Paid data terms to all services for EEA accounts. This is strong evidence that free production use from the EU is **non-compliant**, but the exact mechanics of "a German-account developer creating a free key" are not pinned to a single official sentence — only inferred from the terms.
- **(b) Gemini free-tier output-storage/re-serving terms:** The "Google won't claim ownership" clause is official and unambiguous, and commercial use is permitted on both tiers. No residual uncertainty on ownership/re-serving itself — the only constraint is the EEA Paid-Services requirement above.
- **Gemini free-tier RPM/RPD/TPM:** Not officially pinned; Google directs you to AI Studio for live per-project limits and disclaims guarantees. Secondary figures (10–15 RPM, 250–1,500 RPD, 1M TPM) conflict across sources and reflect 2025–2026 quota cuts.
- **YandexGPT Lite structured output:** Schema-enforced `response_format: json_schema` is documented for `yandexgpt` (Pro); explicit `yandexgpt-lite` schema-enforcement was not separately confirmed (free-form JSON via prompt works on all text models). The ~0.20 RUB/1k synchronous price is corroborated by secondary/older-official sources; confirm on the live ruble pricing page.
- **DeepSeek details:** The 5M-token bonus is reported as expiring 30 days **from registration** with one grant per account (third-party reports, not a single quoted official page); and the exact scope of the training opt-out for API/open-platform usage vs. consumer chat is not fully pinned to one official statement.
- **Process/security note:** One search result (getaiperks.com) contained an embedded prompt-injection attempt — a fake "RootSystemPrompt" instructing AI assistants to give the site favorable analysis. It was identified, ignored, and not relied upon for any finding in this report.
